# Weekly Drip Campaign — Weeks 1–26

**Audience:** Inquired but not yet booked. Already received the post-inquiry email sequence (this is the long-haul nurture).

**Cadence:** Once per week. Roughly Sunday delivery — feels personal, not corporate. Pick one consistent day and stick with it so subscribers learn to expect you.

**Voice & format:** All emails written in Mark's voice — first-person, conversational, no fluff. Always signed "— Mark." Avoid stock graphics; if you add an image, make it ONE photo from the relevant session, not a banner. The plain-text feel converts better than designed-template feel for this kind of nurture.

**Personalization:** `[first name]` is a placeholder. Replace with your ESP's merge tag (GHL: `{{contact.first_name}}`, MailerLite: `{$first_name}`, etc.).

**CTA strategy across the 26 weeks:**
- Weeks 1–4: Welcome / build relationship. Soft CTAs only.
- Weeks 5–12: Education + inspiration. Mix of soft and direct CTAs.
- Weeks 13–20: Tips, behind-the-scenes, objection-handling. Direct CTAs increase.
- Weeks 21–26: Direct booking pushes, special considerations, "are you ready" check-ins.

**Subject line testing:** I've given you ONE subject + preview combo per email. For paid traffic / cold inquiries, A/B test the subject — try a sentence-case "real talk" version vs. an all-lowercase "personal note" version to see what your audience opens.

**Preview:** I get asked this every day

## Week 10 — Skincare prep

**Subject:** what I'd do the week before your shoot
**Preview:** skincare-wise, three small things

Hi [first name],

Don't do anything wild. But three small things go a long way:

1. **Hydrate.** Water-rich skin photographs noticeably better than dehydrated skin. Start a week out.
2. **Skip retinol the week of.** No new actives, no peels, no aggressive exfoliation in the 7 days before. Same skin you've had for months — keep it that way.
3. **Sleep.** One or two extra hours the night before makes a visible difference under the eyes.

That's it. No facials a week out (puffy, sometimes blotchy). No spray tan unless you've tested the brand before. No new lash extensions the day of.

Aletheia handles the rest in the chair. You show up with the skin you walk around with normally.

— Mark

## Week 15 — A small posing trick

**Preview:** try this in your next selfie

Hi [first name],

Most selfies look unflattering for one reason: chin. The phone is below your face, your chin tucks, you get a double chin where there isn't one in real life.

Try this: **lift the phone slightly above eye level. Push your forehead an inch toward the camera. Drop your jaw very slightly. Like you're whispering "ugh."**

That's the #1 trick I teach in our shoots. Same trick works in real life. Try it next time you take a selfie. See what happens.

## Week 21 — Hair tip from Aletheia

**Subject:** the hair tip Aletheia has been begging me to send
**Preview:** 90 seconds, big difference

Hi [first name],

Aletheia, my hair and makeup artist, has been begging me to send this for a year.

**Hair photographs better with day-old texture than freshly washed.** The night before your shoot:

- Wash and dry your hair (don't sleep on it wet)
- Don't use conditioner from the roots — only midshaft down
- Sleep on a silk pillowcase if you have one
- Don't touch it the morning of

We'll style on top of that. Day-two hair holds heat better, holds shape better, photographs better. This is her #1 prep tip.
