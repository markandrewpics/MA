Common hesitations

Yes, you can. Do this.

"Where is the studio?"
We have a beautiful, private studio in the Habersham Marketplace, Beaufort, SC. The exact address is shared after you're accepted.
"Why are you offering complimentary sessions?"
We're updating our studio's viewing room and need women to photograph for new sample albums, wall prints, and other products to be featured in studio. In exchange for being featured, your session is on us.
"How long will the session last?"
About 4 hours total. That includes hair and makeup, shooting, editing, and viewing your images the same day. We do a lot in a short amount of time — and you're never rushed.
"Can my partner be photographed too?"
Yes — we love photographing couples. They're welcome to join your shoot at no extra cost.
"Can I bring a friend?"
Yes — your friend is welcome to stay during hair and makeup. Aletheia stays during the entire shoot to assist with wardrobe and touchups.
"Do I have to buy any photos?"
No. You have no obligation to purchase anything. You'll see 150+ edited images at your viewing and choose what (if anything) you want.
"What if I want to buy keepsakes?"
Optional. Art pieces start at $400. Most clients invest around $1,000 in finished art and digital files. Entirely your call — and you have a $100 print credit to put toward it.
"I'm not photogenic."
Almost everyone says this. It's not true — it's just that you've never been photographed by someone who's used to photographing every figure, every age, every personality. Mark has spent a decade in studio with thousands of women across every body type and every season of life, and he poses every shot — head, shoulders, hands, hips, breath — so you never have to "figure out what to do."
Is it awkward?
Not the way we run it. The studio is private, the team is small (Mark + Aletheia), and most women say the awkwardness they expected never showed up. Within the first 30 minutes, you'll be laughing.
"I don't know what to bring."
That's exactly what the planning call is for. We send you a full wardrobe and prep guide after you book, then walk through everything together on a 30-minute video call before the shoot.
"I don't know what to wear."
Most women say this — and almost every one ends up obsessed with what we choose together. We pick wardrobe based on your body, your colors, and the vibe you're going for. Nothing is off the rack and ill-fitting.
"I don't know how to pose."
You're not supposed to. Mark poses every shot — head, shoulders, hands, hips, breath — frame by frame. You never have to "figure out what to do." That's his entire job.
