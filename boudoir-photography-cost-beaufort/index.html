How Much Does Boudoir Photography Cost in Beaufort, SC?

Boudoir photography in Beaufort, SC and the SC Lowcountry ranges from approximately $600 to $7,500+ total, depending on the studio's experience tier. Entry-level session-only studios start around $600. Mid-range studios with image packages run $1,200 to $3,000. Luxury full-experience studios — including planning consultations, hand-made albums, and same-day reveal sessions — typically run $3,000 and up.

Average Boudoir Photography Cost in Beaufort, SC

Boudoir clients in Beaufort, SC and the SC Lowcountry typically spend between $600 and $7,500 total, including the session, hair and makeup, and any images or products kept. Pricing is driven primarily by the studio's experience tier — session-only versus full-experience — and by the type of finished products selected, such as digital files, fine art prints, or hand-made albums.

Entry-Level Boudoir Studios ($600–$1,200)

Entry-level studios in the Beaufort, SC area typically charge $600 to $1,200 total. These are usually session-only or session-plus-digital-pack, with limited retouching, no hand-made albums, and a digital gallery delivered weeks later. Hair and makeup may or may not be included. This tier is best for clients on a tight budget or those testing whether boudoir is for them.

Mid-Range Boudoir Studios ($1,200–$3,000)

Mid-range Lowcountry studios fall between $1,200 and $3,000 total. These typically include the session, professional hair and makeup, a small selection of retouched images (5–15), and limited print or product options. Reveal sessions are sometimes online, sometimes in-person. This tier suits clients who want professionally retouched images without the full luxury experience.

Luxury Full-Experience Studios ($3,000+)

Luxury full-experience studios in the Beaufort, SC area charge $3,000 and up. These include a pre-session planning consultation, professional hair and makeup, a full-day shoot, a same-day reveal session, custom hand-made albums, wall art, and significantly more time per image in retouching. Mark Andrew Boudoir falls into this tier — it is a full-experience luxury studio in Beaufort, SC.

What Factors Affect Boudoir Photography Pricing?

Boudoir photography pricing in Beaufort, SC is driven by six factors: session length, number of outfits and looks, hair and makeup, studio versus on-location shooting, the type of finished products selected (albums, wall art, digital files), and the time required to retouch each image.

Session Length

A Mark Andrew Boudoir session is a full-day experience. Hair and makeup begins at 9 AM, photography starts at 10:30 AM, the shoot wraps by noon, and your same-day reveal session begins at 1 PM. This single-day format avoids the back-and-forth of a multi-week timeline — you walk out with chosen images and an order placed before the day is over.

Number of Outfits

The standard $499 booking retainer includes up to three outfit changes. Each outfit creates a distinct visual story — a corset, a sweater set, a partner's button-down — and influences how many final images you'll have to choose from. Additional looks may be added by request during your planning consultation.

Hair and Makeup Services

Professional hair and makeup runs $200 to $250 and is paid directly to your makeup artist. This includes a full-face camera-ready makeup application, light hair styling (such as loose curls or waves), and false lashes — everything needed to look polished on camera under studio lighting. Boudoir-specific styling is more intensive than typical makeup; under bright lights and high-resolution lenses, untrained makeup tends to wash out or appear uneven.

Studio vs On-Location Sessions

Mark Andrew Boudoir sessions take place in a private studio in Beaufort, SC. The studio includes multiple shooting environments — clean editorial backdrops, dramatic curtains, and styled vignettes — so you don't pay for travel or location fees, and the lighting is fully controlled.

Albums and Printed Artwork

Tangible products are the largest pricing variable. Hand-made albums range from 8x8 (Silver tier) to 12x12 (Platinum and Diamond). Wall art ranges from 16x24 ($830) to 30x40 ($2,190). Print boxes, fine art prints, and the Dream Album Upgrade ($480 — adds an acrylic cover, a 2-image folio, and all-black-and-white versions) are all available individually.

Retouching and Editing Time

Every image you select is professionally retouched. Skin is smoothed without losing texture, color is graded for a consistent editorial look, and stray hairs and undergarment lines are cleaned up. Retouching is what separates professional boudoir from a phone-camera shoot — and it's why image purchases are priced as final art, not raw files.

Is Boudoir Photography Worth It?

For most clients, boudoir photography is worth the investment because it produces something a phone camera cannot — a curated, retouched, professionally lit body of work that captures who you are right now. Beyond the images, the experience itself rebuilds confidence and self-perception. Most clients describe the reveal session as transformational.

Confidence and Self-Empowerment

Most clients arrive nervous and leave standing differently. There's a reason boudoir is sometimes called "self-portraiture for women who never see themselves" — it's a single afternoon of being photographed the way you want to be remembered, with full creative direction and a team treating you with intention.

Popular Occasions for Boudoir Sessions

Common reasons clients book a session: an upcoming wedding (bridal boudoir), an anniversary, a birthday milestone (especially 30, 40, 50), a divorce or post-divorce reset, a body transformation worth celebrating, a deployment gift for a partner, or simply a self-celebration with no external occasion at all. You don't need a reason. Wanting one is the reason.

Why Many Clients Say the Experience Is Transformational

Clients consistently say the same thing in feedback: they didn't realize they could look like that. The combination of professional makeup, posing direction, lighting, and retouching produces images that feel surprising the first time you see them. Many clients book again within 12 months — which is why the Diamond and Platinum collections include a Client For Life Pass.

Privacy and Discretion in Boudoir Photography

Privacy is foundational to professional boudoir. Your photos are never shared, posted, or used for portfolio promotion without your written consent. Image delivery happens through a private password-protected gallery, and the studio operates on a strict opt-in model: every image stays private unless you specifically grant permission to share it.

Who Sees Your Photos

By default, only you and Mark Andrew Boudoir see your images. You may choose to share with a partner, but no third-party ever sees your gallery. The studio does not post client images to social media, the website, or marketing materials without a separate signed model release.

Image Delivery and Privacy Policies

All image delivery happens through a password-protected private gallery — the same platform used by professional fine art photographers. No images are sent over email or text. Galleries can be set to expire, password-rotated, or deleted at your request.

Client Permission for Portfolio Use

If a client wants to be featured on the website or in social media, a separate model release agreement is signed. This is always opt-in. Clients who decline are never used for promotional purposes — and most clients decline, which is completely expected.

Boudoir Photography Cost FAQs

How much should I budget for a boudoir session in Beaufort, SC?
A safe budget for first-time boudoir clients in Beaufort, SC is $1,500 to $2,500 total. This covers the $499 booking retainer, $200 to $250 in hair and makeup, and a typical first-time image investment of $1,000 to $2,000 for a small album, a digital file or two, or a print box. Clients building a full collection from the published tiers should budget $3,800 to $8,000 total.
Do I have to buy any images at the reveal?
No. There is no studio minimum. The $499 booking retainer covers the photoshoot itself, your planning call, and your prep guides. If you decide at the reveal that you don't want to take anything home, you don't have to. That said, almost every client ends up choosing at least a small selection — once you see the images, the decision is usually whether to take some or all of them.
Do you offer payment plans for boudoir collections?
Yes. Mark Andrew Boudoir offers third-party payment plans through Afterpay, Klarna, and Affirm. For orders above $1,000, the studio also offers an in-house 6-month interest-free payment plan with $1,000 down — available regardless of credit approval status.
Is the $499 booking retainer refundable?
The booking retainer is non-refundable, but it is transferable to a new date with sufficient notice if you need to reschedule. The retainer covers the studio, the planning consultation, prep guides, and the reserved full-day shoot — costs that are committed once your date is locked.
Are there hidden fees?
No. The pricing structure is: $499 booking retainer + $200 to $250 hair and makeup (paid directly to your makeup artist) + whatever you choose to invest in images and products at the reveal. There are no surcharges, editing fees, or delivery charges.
What should I wear to my session?
Wardrobe is covered in your prep guide and your planning call. Most clients bring 3 to 5 outfits across the three included looks: a soft/romantic look, a confident/sexy look, and a "yourself" look (a sweater, partner's shirt, jersey). For full guidance, see What to Wear for Your Boudoir Session.
What if I don't know how to pose?
Posing direction is included in every shoot. Mark guides every angle and adjustment in real time — most first-time clients don't pose themselves at all. For more detail, see How to Prepare for Your Boudoir Session.
I'm not sure if boudoir is right for me. Is it awkward?
This is one of the most common pre-booking concerns. The short answer: it isn't. The studio is a private, controlled environment, the team is small, and the experience is structured around your comfort. For a fuller answer, see Is Boudoir Photography Awkward?
How many images will I receive?
Image counts depend on the collection chosen. Silver: 20 images. Gold: 35 images. Platinum: 45 images. Diamond: all images from your shoot (typically 75 to 100). À la carte options start at 2 digital images for $590.
How long does a boudoir session take?
A full-day boudoir session at Mark Andrew Boudoir runs from 9 AM (hair and makeup) through approximately 2 PM (end of reveal). Photography itself runs 10:30 AM to noon. The reveal session begins at 1 PM after a one-hour break.
I want to lose weight before booking. Should I wait?
Many clients say this — and a year later they say the same thing. Boudoir photography is for who you are right now. Professional posing, lighting, and retouching are designed for every body. The clients who hesitate longest are usually the most stunned at their reveal session.
My partner doesn't want me to book. What now?
Most clients who book a boudoir session are doing it for themselves first — not as a gift for a partner. The experience is private and the images are entirely yours. Partner reactions tend to shift after the reveal, but the decision to book is yours alone.
