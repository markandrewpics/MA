The Honest Answer

What is boudoir, actually?

Boudoir is a professional portrait session where you're the subject, the muse, and the entire point. Think old-Hollywood glamour — bold lighting, statement jewelry, painted backdrops, fur coats, gowns — not trends that will look dated in five years. My rule: these should look so timeless your granddaughter says, "look how good Grandma looked."

It's tasteful and classy by default — you decide how glamorous or how spicy it gets. And no, you don't need modeling experience. I pose every client head to toe, hands to chin, in every single shot. Most women who walk through my door have never done anything like this. That's normal. That's the job.

Who It's For

Real women. Every body.

Birthdays and milestones. Brides making a wedding gift. Women coming out the other side of divorce, weight change, cancer, motherhood — or no reason at all except that it's time to feel like yourself again. Ages 18 to 80. No "boudoir body" required, because there's no such thing — posing and light are my problem, not yours.
