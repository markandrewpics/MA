Section 02

The Business in One Paragraph
Mark Andrew Boudoir is an award-winning luxury boudoir photography studio that gives women a full-day, one-on-one portrait experience — professional hair and makeup, head-to-toe posing direction, and a same-day reveal of their images. The work is built for the woman who has never felt photogenic and helps her become "the woman she didn't know she could be: confident, undeniable, and deeply seen." It is not really a photo session — it is a transformation, and tangible proof of it.
"Be photographed the way you want to be remembered."— Brand tagline

Section 03

The Founder — Mark Boughton

Mark Boughton is the photographer, owner, and the person clients actually work with from first hello to final album. He is a U.S. Marine Corps veteran, and that background is central to the brand: discipline, steadiness, and a deep sense of responsibility for the people who trust him. He has run the studio since 2019.

A defining principle of the business is that the person who sells is the person who delivers. Mark personally guides the consultation, the planning, the shoot, and the reveal. Clients are not handed off.

In his own words (client-facing bio)

"My mission is to help every woman see herself the way the people who love her already do — powerful, beautiful, and worthy of being celebrated."

Mark also builds a personal brand ("Mark Andrew") as a photographer, veteran, educator and speaker. It crossed 50 million views of content in a single year. The two brands work together: the company brand books shoots; the personal brand builds authority and draws people who eventually think, "I want to do a shoot with him." His story is the differentiator.

Section 04

What We Do — Services

The core product is the full-day boudoir session: one client per day, in a private studio, with everything handled for her.

The boudoir experience includes

  • A 30-minute vision planning call after booking — wardrobe, color schemes, props, and what to expect
  • A personalized Style Guide prep document
  • Head-to-toe posing direction — Mark poses every shot; no modeling or camera experience needed
  • A 3-outfit, ~90-minute photoshoot
  • A same-day image reveal of 130–160 fully edited images
  • Hand-retouching on every selected image
  • Delivery through a private, password-protected gallery, with a strict opt-in model release

Hair & makeup is an add-on — not included

Professional hair & makeup is available on shoot day for $200, paid directly to the artist (full face, lashes, light hairstyling). It is an optional add-on on every session and is not included in the session fee or any package.

Tangible products

  • Hand-made fine-art albums (a flagship, heirloom-quality piece)
  • Gallery wall art and fine-art prints
  • Print boxes
  • Digital image files

Genres offered

Boudoir is the flagship. The studio also offers glamour, couples boudoir, and bridal boudoir (a wedding-day gift).

Section 06

The Client Experience — Step by Step

The whole experience is designed to remove every reason a woman might feel lost, judged, or alone — and to turn nervousness into confidence. The arc the brand returns to again and again: she arrives nervous, she leaves in tears (the good kind).

  1. Inquiry & consultation — A no-pressure conversation to walk through pricing, the process, and what her session could look like. "The hardest part is the first message. After that, I take care of the rest."
  2. Booking & retainer — She reserves her date with the booking retainer. A Style Guide and prep resources follow by email.
  3. Vision planning call — A 30-minute call to plan wardrobe, color schemes, props, and personal touches so she arrives prepared and excited.
  4. Shoot day — Professional hair & makeup first (optional $200 add-on), then a relaxed 3-outfit, ~90-minute session with full posing direction. One client per day; the studio is entirely hers.
  5. Lunch break — A pause while the images are prepared for viewing.
  6. Same-day reveal & ordering — She sees 130–160 fully edited images the same day and chooses her favorites and products. Most clients invest $1,000–$2,000. No minimum purchase.
  7. Delivery — Hand-retouched images delivered via a private, password-protected gallery, plus any albums, wall art, or prints she ordered.

Wardrobe guidance the studio gives

  • Bring options, not one outfit — decisions get made together on the day.
  • Lingerie is not required; well-chosen swimsuits and personal pieces photograph beautifully.
  • Bring something with personal meaning (a robe, jewelry, a meaningful item).
  • Match outfit color to contrast with the set, so it pops.
  • Wear loose clothing to the studio so there are no elastic marks; bring an easy-to-remove top so hair & makeup stay perfect.

Section 08

The Ideal Client (Avatar)

Every piece of content, every script, and every conversation is built for one woman. If a message doesn't speak to her, it doesn't get made.

She has spent her life building something — a career, a business, a family, a reputation — and giving generously to everyone around her. Now she's at a milestone: a big birthday, a divorce, a major achievement, a fitness goal, an anniversary. She has the means to do something beautiful for herself and, for the first time, she's ready to actually do it. She's wanted this for years; insecurity and putting herself last kept getting in the way. Not anymore.

Demographics

AgePrimarily 40–55 (sweet spot 42–52); welcomes roughly 30–70
Who she isProfessional, business owner, executive, or established — financially independent
Income levelHousehold income roughly $150,000+; core client $150K–$300K, with a high-net-worth tier above $300K for premium collections & travel shoots
Job / business typeSenior professionals, executives, and business owners — entrepreneurs, healthcare and legal professionals, real estate agents, and corporate leaders
LifestyleLives well and intentionally; med spas, boutique fitness, travel, fine dining; buys quality over price
Booking typeSelf-gift (treating herself) or a gift booked by a partner or family member

What she's quietly going through

  • Self-conscious about her body; hasn't felt truly beautiful in a long time
  • Exhausted from giving to everyone else; doing something just for herself feels almost foreign
  • Secretly wants to feel like a movie star — to be the subject for once, not the one holding everything together

Her top pain points

  1. She doesn't feel beautiful in her own skin. She's self-conscious about her body and hasn't liked a photo of herself in years.
  2. She's wanted to do this for years but keeps putting it off. Between always putting everyone else first and her own insecurity, "someday" never comes — even though she's more than earned it.
  3. She's afraid it won't work for her. She's not a model, doesn't know how to pose or what to wear, and quietly worries she'll spend the money and hate the results — or that she's "too old" or "not the right body type."

What she's really buying

She'll say she wants beautiful photos. She is actually buying freedom — freedom from the story she's told herself about how she looks, and the confidence that comes from doing something just for her. She arrives at Point A (nervous, unsure she deserves this) and leaves at Point C (lit up, transformed, carrying herself differently). She doesn't know how to get from A to C — that's why she hires us. We are Point B. She also wants something tangible to hold onto: an album that lets her look back at 65 and say, "that's who I was, and I was beautiful."

How she decides

  • She is not price-shopping — she is trust-shopping. She wants to feel safe and in expert hands.
  • She is moved most by other women's stories — women who looked like her, were terrified, and are now in happy tears.
  • She may take weeks or months to decide; once she trusts, she commits fully.

What makes her pull the trigger and buy

  • A limited-time sale or promotion we run through the year — a real deadline finally overrides her habit of putting it off.
  • A model call — when we need someone to model a new set, backdrop, or be featured in studio samples. Being "chosen" gives her permission and quiets the guilt about spending on herself.
  • A life milestone — a big birthday, divorce, anniversary, empty nest, or finally hitting a fitness goal.
  • Trust tipping over — after weeks or months of watching our transformations, "that's for other women" becomes "if she can do it, so can I."
  • The risk feeling low enough — payment plans, a no-minimum reveal, and our reputation make it feel safe to say yes.
  • A nudge from someone she trusts, or simply giving herself permission: "it's finally my turn."

Secondary ICP — the devoted mom reclaiming herself new

A mom, roughly 30s to mid-40s, with two to four kids somewhere between elementary and high school. She's in the thick of it — drop-offs, sports, meals, laundry, keeping the whole house running — and she pours every ounce of herself into her kids and her family.

Somewhere in all of it, she lost touch with herself. She can't remember the last time she felt soft, feminine, or beautiful instead of just "Mom." She adores her family — she's just slowly disappeared underneath the caregiving, the cleaning, and the constant giving. What she wants is to feel like a woman again, not only a mother. A boudoir session becomes her permission to put herself first for one day and remember who she is underneath it all. She usually books as a gift to herself after a long season, or her husband gifts it because he wants her to feel special and chosen again.

Who we're NOT for (anti-ICP) new

We're intentionally not for everyone:

  • Anyone who believes this kind of photography is wrong or immodest. People with very traditional or conservative views about how a woman should present herself, who'd see boudoir as inappropriate rather than empowering. We're not here to change their mind — they're simply not our client.
  • Anyone who doesn't believe in celebrating themselves this way. If a woman doing something bold and feminine just for herself feels self-indulgent or "wrong" to them, the experience won't land.
  • Bargain and discount shoppers. People hunting for the cheapest mini-session or "just the digitals." Our client is trust-shopping, not price-shopping.
  • Anyone looking for something this isn't. This is an artful, tasteful, empowering portrait experience — not anything explicit or escort-adjacent. Anyone inquiring with that expectation is firmly not our customer.
  • People who want a fake, heavily-Photoshopped or AI version of themselves. We celebrate the real woman in front of the camera.

Section 10

Messaging — Language That Lands & Language to Avoid

Language that lands

  • "Celebrate your next chapter" · "You've earned this"
  • "You don't have to know how to pose — that's what we're here for"
  • "Women just like you came in nervous and left in happy tears"
  • "This is for you. Just you."
  • "The most beautiful version of you is already there — we just show her to the world"

Language to avoid

  • Anything implying she needs to "fix" herself
  • Leading with product specs (album sizes, print options) before the transformation
  • Young, trendy, or club-adjacent language aimed at a 25-year-old
  • Anything that makes the shoot feel body-type exclusive or inaccessible
  • High-pressure, salesy phrasing

Hook formulas the brand uses

  • "This is your sign to have a photoshoot for [reason / person / occasion]"
  • "This is how I photograph women"
  • Real-over-AI positioning: "We still prefer real magic over AI"

Section 11

Core Values & What We Stand For

World-class craft

Magazine-quality images, made with complete intentionality. The standard is work that belongs on a cover.

Trust, safety & discretion

Clients are physically and emotionally vulnerable. Trust is non-negotiable: discretion with images, strict opt-in usage, never making a client look bad for views.

Supportive & judgment-free

The entire experience is engineered so she never feels lost, judged, or alone — from consult to wardrobe to reveal.

Real over AI

Human artistry and genuine moments over synthetic shortcuts — a deliberate market position.

Lines we won't cross

  • Never degrade a client or anyone else.
  • Never use behind-the-scenes or client footage that makes someone look bad — even if it would go viral.
  • The rule: if a client would be embarrassed to see it, it doesn't go up.

Section 13

Frequently Asked Questions (Client-Facing)

These are the real questions clients ask, with the studio's answers. Use them directly when responding to prospective clients.

Is boudoir only for certain body types?

No. There is no "boudoir body — there is only yours." Every body, every age, every skin tone is welcome and photographs beautifully.

Do I need modeling experience?

None at all. Mark gives head-to-toe posing direction for every single shot.

Will I have to do anything I'm uncomfortable with?

Never. You set the comfort level, and you can stop or adjust anytime.

How long does a session take?

A full day: hair & makeup, a ~90-minute / 3-outfit shoot, a lunch break, and the same-day reveal.

How many images will I get?

You'll see 130–160 fully edited images at your same-day reveal and choose your favorites.

Do I have to buy images at the reveal?

There's no minimum purchase — what you take home is up to you. Most clients invest $1,000–$2,000.

Is the $499 retainer refundable?

It's non-refundable but transferable, and it reserves your full day plus all the prep.

Do you offer payment plans?

Yes — Affirm, Klarna, Afterpay, and an in-house 6-month interest-free plan.

Will my photos be posted online?

Only with your explicit permission. Image use is strictly opt-in, and your gallery is private and password-protected.

I want to lose weight first — should I wait?

Don't wait. Boudoir is for who you are right now; the clients who hesitate longest are usually the most stunned at their reveal.

My partner isn't sure about it — what now?

This is for you first. Let's talk it through — no pressure, just a conversation.

Is it awkward?

Most clients arrive nervous and leave standing differently. The most common line at the reveal is "that was so much more fun than I thought."

Content & Marketing Strategy

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)

Content strategy follows AEO, taught by Mark's mentor Sal Cincotta. The goal is to be the answer that AI systems and Google surface for people researching boudoir. Pages answer real buyer questions (cost, what to wear, how to prepare, is it awkward, is it worth it), lead with a tight 40–60 word answer, and use question-based headings, topical clusters, internal linking, and schema markup.

Two-brand strategy

  • Mark Andrew Boudoir (company brand) — speaks to prospective clients; goal is booked shoots
  • Mark Andrew (personal brand) — photographer, veteran, educator, speaker; goal is authority, audience, and speaking/education opportunities

Channels & cadence

  • Instagram — optimized profile grid (mix of transformation, behind-the-scenes, personal, educational) and three conversion-focused pinned posts
  • Blog at blog.markandrewboudoir.com — AEO cluster posts
  • Content pillars: client transformations · behind-the-scenes craft · personal story · educational (what to wear / prep) · hot takes
  • Metrics that matter: saves and shares over raw views — "views get reach; saves and shares get buyers"
